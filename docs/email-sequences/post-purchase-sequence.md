## Email P2: Deep Value (Day 3)

**Subject:** The one technique worth the price alone
**Preview:** Why parallel compression changes everything (and how most people get it wrong)

If you've tried the parallel compression setup from my last email, you already heard the difference. Now let me show you where most people go wrong with it.

The mistake: using parallel compression on everything.

Parallel compression works best on sources with strong transients - drums, percussion, sometimes bass. When you apply it to vocals or pads, you're amplifying room noise and breath sounds along with the signal. The result is a mix that feels louder but also noisier and more fatigued.

Here's the refined approach I use:

For drums: Heavy compression (1176-style, all-buttons mode if available), blended at -10 to -15dB. This adds density and sustain to the shells without flattening the cymbals.

For bass: Gentler ratio (4:1), slower attack to let the pluck through. Blend at -8 to -12dB. Adds consistency without killing the groove.

For vocals: Skip parallel compression entirely. Use serial compression instead - two compressors in sequence, each doing 3-4dB of reduction. Much more transparent.

For the full mix bus: This is where the Advanced Edition goes deep. Chapter 5 covers bus processing order, why compression before EQ sounds different than EQ before compression, and how to use the SSL Fusion's approach as a template even if you're working entirely with plugins.

The book gives you the full picture. This email gives you the filter for applying it.
